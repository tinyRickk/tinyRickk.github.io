About Me

I am a PhD candidate at University of New South Wales, Sydney, working in the field of Experimental Quantum Communications. My research interests encompass Quantum Information and Computation, Quantum Cryptography, Machine Learning, Experimental Quantum Optics, and Theoretical Physics.

My research explores implementing a free-space quantum key distribution (QKD) protocol that achieves practical secret key generation rates while maintaining security levels closest possible to information-theoretic security.

Before joining the PhD program at UNSW, I worked at Qulabs Software (India) Pvt. Ltd., a quantum communications and computations-based startup, where I served as a team lead in the Blind Quantum Computation (BQC) group for 1.5 years. My work focused on developing a delegated BQC scheme for fully classical clients under quantum-resistant computational security requirements. The first working version was successfully deployed in September 2023.

I completed my Master's degree from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Roorkee in 2022, where I first discovered the field of quantum information and commenced my research journey.

When not engaged in academic pursuits, I enjoy fitness training, calisthenics, beach outings, and exploring remote locations through spontaneous road trips.
